VHDL decoder — every token in oricatmos.vhd

This card lists every VHDL construct, operator, and literal form that appears anywhere in oricatmos.vhd, with its meaning and the lesson that teaches it. Keep it open beside the source: when you hit a keyword or symbol on any line and aren't sure what it does, find it here. If a line contains only tokens in these tables, you can decode that line in full. (Syntax only — what a given signal does functionally is in 01b-oricatmos-vhd-understanding.md.)

How to decode any line of oricatmos.vhd:
  1. Where am I? Inside a PROCESS (sequential; <= is clocked) or out in the architecture body between BEGIN and END RTL (concurrent; <= is continuous, like SV's assign)? This is the single most important question in VHDL.
  2. Is it a declaration? Before the architecture's BEGIN: SIGNAL/COMPONENT. In the entity: a PORT.
  3. Is it an instantiation? label : ENTITY work.X PORT MAP(...) or label : comp PORT MAP(...) — then it's ports, not an expression.
  4. Otherwise break the expression into tokens and look each up: WHEN/ELSE, operators, casts, slices, &.
⇄ The two symbol traps vs. SystemVerilog: in VHDL, & is concatenation (SV's { }), and bitwise-AND is the word AND (SV's &). And <= means continuous assignment outside a PROCESS but clocked/non-blocking inside one. Check these two every time.
